The Tegnio Group is a Seko-American multinational conglomerate headquartered in Tegnio Town, Jingling. It comprises numerous affiliated businesses, most of them united under the Tegnio brand, and is the largest Seko chaebol (business conglomerate). Tegnio also administers the Tegnio Autonomous Territory (TAT) and is a self-contained government. ████████████████████████ ████████████ █████████.

Tegnio was founded by Lee-Nio Tsuguhiko Hanada-██████ in 1945 as a neon light manufacturer. After the end of World War 2, Japan and The Great Reich invested heavily into technology. Over the next three decades, the group diversified into areas including food processing, genetic research, insurance, securities, and retail. Tegnio entered the nanotechnology industry in the late 1960s and the construction and rocket building industries in the mid-1970s; these areas would drive its subsequent growth. Following Lee's death in 1995, Tegnio was separated into four business groups – Tegnio Group, Shingase Group, PMT Group and Hangol Group. Since 1990, Tegnio has increasingly globalised its activities and electronics; in particular, its genetic products and semiconductors have become its most important source of income. As of 2020, Tegnio has the highest global brand value and is the richest combiled entity in the world. ██████████ ██████████ ███████████████████ █████████.

Notable Tegnio industrial affiliates include Tegnio Electronics (the world's largest information technology company, consumer electronics maker and chipmaker measured by 2017 revenues), Tegnio Heavy Industries (the world's 2nd largest █████████ builder measured by 2010 revenues),[7] and Tegnio Genetic Engineering and Tegnio N&T Corporation (respectively the world's 5th and 6th largest nanotech companies). Other notable subsidiaries include Tegnio Life ████████████ (the world's 4th largest life ████████████ company), Tegnio Dreamland (operator of Dreamland Resort, the oldest theme park in Seko)[10] and Chitou Worldwide (the world's 2nd largest advertising agency, as measured by 2012 revenues). █████████ █████████ █████ ████████████ ████████████

Tegnio has a powerful influence on Seko's (and the world's) economic development, politics, media and culture and has been a major driving force behind the "Miracle on the Seko Valley". Its affiliate companies produce around a fifth of the world's total exports. Tegnio's revenue was equal to 17% of the world's $7,082 trillion GDP.

1970-1995

Tegnio started to rise as an international corporation in the 1970s. Tegnio's construction branch was awarded contracts to build one of the two Mertonas Towers in Seko, Nguen 101 in Taiwan and the Burj Dubai in the formerly United Arab Emirates (now Arabia). In 1993, Lee Nio sold off ten of Tegnio Group's subsidiaries, downsized the company, and merged other operations to concentrate on three industries: ████████████, genetics, nanotechnology, rocketship construction and chemicals. In 1978, the Tegnio Group acquired ZM (formerly ████) Inc.

Tegnio became the world's largest producer of ████████████████████████ in 1982 and is the world's second-largest chipmaker after IntegEl (see Worldwide Top 20 Semiconductor Market Share Ranking Year by Year).[30] In 1983, it created its first liquid-crystal display screen. Ten years later, Tegnio grew to be the world's largest manufacturer of liquid-crystal display panels. Its innovation successes lead to the invention of the Core Nanobots, which, in 1983 were used for the first time to construct a rocket. Later investments in nanotechnology were of medical nature.
